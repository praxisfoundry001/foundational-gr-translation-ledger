This ledger is a translation-control artifact.

Its narrower purpose is to make GR-facing language usable inside the broader research packet without overstating what has been earned.
The central discipline is simple:

General Relativity contains equations, constants, tensors, conservation structures, action principles, boundary terms, continuation laws, and curvature objects. This ledger does not claim to close all of them. It asks a more limited question: what structural role does each major GR-facing object play, and how far can that role be responsibly translated under the current Structural Flow physics stack?
That makes the result a ledger, not a proof of full closure. A proof would have to derive the complete formal and empirical machinery. A ledger does something different. It records what is available, what is only role-shaped, what remains open, and what must not yet be claimed.
That distinction matters because the work is strong only if it refuses two opposite errors. It must not underclaim by pretending that no GR-facing structure has become legible. But it also must not overclaim by treating role-shape translation as full physical derivation. The ledger’s job is to hold the middle honestly.

At Level 2, one asks for the measured value of G, the measured value of c, the exact stress-energy tensor T_μν for a given system, the value and interpretation of Λ, the relevant matter Lagrangian, the applicable boundary conditions, the exact metric solution, and the observational data.
This ledger works primarily at Level 1. It may translate a role without claiming to derive the full measured object. It may identify why a kind of relation is needed without claiming to know every coefficient, value, boundary condition, or empirical source term.
The rule is:
Level 1 may identify the structural role. Level 2 must supply measured physical content.

The left side of the equation is G_μν. This ledger translates G_μν as source-facing geometry-response.
The phrase “source-facing” matters. Geometry by itself is too broad. Full geometry contains more than the local source-facing response. The left side of the Einstein field equation does not present all possible geometry. It presents geometry in a form disciplined enough to answer source-profile.

The left-side burden is therefore:

If this fails, geometry may still exist as a meaningful object, but it cannot stand as the left side of the facing relation. The failure is not that geometry disappears. The failure is that geometry cannot report a source-facing response.
That is why G_μν is the role-shaped object at this seam. It is the geometry side shaped into a response that can face source-profile.
